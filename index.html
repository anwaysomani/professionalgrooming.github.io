Dress Code


Dressing definitely has an impact on your personality.Thus, a proper arrangement of clothes needs to be maintained for professional look.

  • Suit and suit-pants
  • Dresses with jacket(if not above)
10%









Clean Clothes


A shabby looking dress does affect your confidence. Before anyone make a judgement, you start to feel disobedient and uncomfortable.

  • Wear clothes of fitting size
  • Always wear an ironed attire
  • Comfort out any extra accesory along with the dress(like belt, cufflink, etc)
20%










Color Coding


One should be automatically noticeable in an interview...but should not force someone to notice them

  • Avoid highly fluorescent colors
  • Dark colored dresses are appreciated
130%








Jewelry


Heavy jewelry piece diverts the interviewers view from the present situation. Avoid it!

  • Wear small, conservative piece of jewelry
  • Avoid multiple rings in hand
  • Avoid expensive, glitzing pieces

AVOID!!!!
140%








Make-Up


Heavy makeup can promise you a monstrous interview.....light makeup with touch of gloss makes one pretty and sustainable.

  • Avoid heavy makeup
  • Avoid mascara too
  • Wear light makeup
50%








Shoes


Prevent falls, and slips. It does not cast a great impression.

  • Avoid shoe of type 'UNCOMFORTABLE to me'
  • Avoid heels above 3-inch
  • Use dark colored footwear
60%








Hair Style


A mind should be heavy...but not with the burden of hair, but with the burden of knowledge. You brain is an information server, not a hair server!!

  • Keep a short hair-cut for the interviews
  • Keep hair out of face
70%









Nails


90% of your daily tasks are done by you hands. And you hand tips consists of the nails. You display actions, you display behaviour, but with an untidy hands, you are bound to be stuck.

  • Clip long nails
  • Use mild colors as nail paints
  • Always keep nail polish-remover in case of need
70%










Fragrance


If you stink, people avoid.
Wanderers and impressers find your fragrance attractive.

  • Avoid strong perfumes.
  • Use mild fragrances.
  • A mini-perfume is recommended to be always presentable.
